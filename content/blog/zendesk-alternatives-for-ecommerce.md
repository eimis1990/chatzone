Zendesk is the enterprise standard for customer support — deep, configurable, and used by huge organisations. That depth is exactly why it's often the wrong tool for an online store: you inherit the complexity (and the cost) of a platform built for 200-agent contact centres when you have a team of three. Here are the alternatives worth considering.

## Why stores look for a Zendesk alternative

- **It's enterprise-shaped.** Zendesk assumes a large support operation — roles, SLAs, complex routing, lots of configuration. A store usually wants "answer my customers well," not a contact-centre platform.
- **AI billed per resolution.** Zendesk's AI charges around $1.50 per automated resolution at the time of writing (committed in blocks). That's a clean outcome-based model, but at store scale it's another meter on top of agent seats and add-ons.
- **Setup overhead.** Standing up Zendesk well is a project. Most stores want to be live this afternoon, not after an implementation.

To be clear: Zendesk is excellent at what it's for. It's just aimed at a much larger buyer than a typical store.

## What to look for instead

- **Store-first, not contact-centre-first** — built around catalog and orders. ([What a good store agent does.](/blog/ai-chatbot-for-online-store))
- **Predictable, flat pricing** — an allowance you can forecast, not seats plus per-resolution fees.
- **Grounded answers + real actions** — order lookup and product search, with an honest "I'm not sure."
- **One-line install + handoff on every plan** — [live the same day, with a clean path to a human](/blog/ai-chatbot-human-handoff).
