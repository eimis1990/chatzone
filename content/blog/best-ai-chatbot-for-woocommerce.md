WooCommerce gives you more control than hosted platforms — and more choice when it comes to chatbots. The catch is that "AI chatbot for WooCommerce" covers two very different things. Sort that out first and the shortlist gets easy.

## Native plugin vs SaaS: the real first decision

- **Native WordPress plugins** (e.g. WoowBot, WPBot, StoreAgent) install straight from the plugin directory and can read your WooCommerce products and orders directly — no API keys. They're cheap and simple, but the AI is often lighter and tied to your WordPress hosting.
- **SaaS tools** (Tidio, Gorgias, Intercom, and Loqara) connect to your store over the WooCommerce REST API. They typically bring stronger AI and a proper shared inbox, and run off your server, at a monthly subscription.

If you want a basic FAQ widget on a budget, a native plugin can be enough. If you want an agent that actually **resolves** support and nudges sales, a SaaS tool with grounded AI is usually the better buy.

## What to look for

- **Reads your real catalog and orders** — so it can answer "where's my order?" and recommend products, not just FAQs. ([How to set that up.](/blog/add-ai-agent-to-woocommerce))
- **Grounded answers** — only from your content, with an honest "I'm not sure" fallback.
- **One-line install** — a single `<script>` (or a snippet plugin), no theme rebuild.
- **Live handoff** — a [clean path to a human](/blog/ai-chatbot-human-handoff) when needed.

## The best options for WooCommerce

**Loqara** — *(us)* AI chat **and** voice, grounded in your content, connected to WooCommerce for live products and orders, added with one line. A real free tier (100 conversations/mo) and conversation-based pricing. Best for stores that want capable AI without per-resolution billing or a heavy helpdesk.

**WoowBot / WPBot** — native WordPress plugins. Inexpensive and simple to install; good if you want a lightweight FAQ/assistant and don't need deep AI resolution.

**Gorgias** — a full e-commerce helpdesk (connects to Woo via API). Best for larger support teams; AI billed per resolution.

**Tidio** — visual flow builder, beginner-friendly; AI is a paid add-on (see [Tidio alternatives](/blog/tidio-alternatives-for-ecommerce)).

## How to choose

If you're a small store: a native plugin or a free-tier SaaS gets you live cheaply — start there and upgrade when volume justifies it. If support is already eating your week, jump to a grounded SaaS agent with real order/catalog actions.
