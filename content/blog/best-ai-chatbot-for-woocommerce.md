## Native plugin vs SaaS: the real first decision

WooCommerce chatbots split into two camps, and they solve different problems:

- **Native WordPress plugins** (e.g. WoowBot, WPBot) install straight from the plugin directory and read your WooCommerce products and orders directly — no API keys, no external account. They're cheap and simple, but the AI is usually lighter and it runs on your own WordPress hosting, so it shares your server's resources.
- **SaaS tools** (Tidio, Gorgias, Re:amaze, Crisp, Zendesk, and Loqara) connect to your store over the WooCommerce REST API and run off your server. They typically bring stronger grounded AI, a proper [shared inbox for human handoff](/blog/ai-chatbot-human-handoff), and richer analytics — at a monthly subscription.

If you want a basic FAQ widget on a budget, a native plugin can be enough. If you want an agent that actually **resolves** support and nudges sales — reading live products and order status, not just matching keywords — a SaaS tool with grounded AI is usually the better buy. The good news: because WooCommerce is open, both camps can plug into the same store, so you can start light and upgrade without re-platforming.

## What makes a chatbot good for WooCommerce

Feature checklists are mostly noise. For a self-hosted Woo store specifically, four things separate a bot that deflects tickets and lifts sales from one that annoys customers into leaving:

**1. It reads your real catalog and orders.** Matching FAQ keywords is table stakes. The agent needs to *search your live products* and *look up a customer's order* through the [WooCommerce connector](/blog/add-ai-agent-to-woocommerce) — that's what kills the endless "where's my order?" tickets and turns a question into a sale.

**2. Grounded answers.** The agent should answer *only* from your real content — policies, FAQs, product data — and say "let me get a human" when it doesn't know. A bot that invents a returns window creates more tickets than it closes. ([Why grounding beats a bigger model.](/blog/ai-chatbot-for-online-store))

**3. A one-line install that respects your stack.** A single `<script>` embed (or a lightweight snippet plugin) means no theme rebuild and nothing heavy bolted onto your WordPress install. You stay in control of your site; the AI runs as a service.

**4. Clean human handoff.** When the bot hits its limit, a person should step in from a shared inbox without the customer repeating themselves.

## How to add an AI chatbot to your WooCommerce store

Whichever tool you pick, the path is roughly the same four steps:

1. **Feed it your knowledge.** Upload your policies, FAQs, and product info — or point it at your site — so answers are grounded in your real content, not a generic model's guesses.
2. **Connect WooCommerce.** Authorise the connector (REST API keys for a SaaS tool, or direct access for a native plugin) so the agent can search live products and look up orders — the part that actually deflects tickets.
3. **Customise the widget.** Colours, launcher, tone, and — if the tool supports it — a voice per language, previewed exactly as customers will see it.
4. **Embed and go live.** Paste the one-line snippet into your theme (or activate the plugin). With a one-line embed like Loqara's, you're answering customers the same afternoon.

On WooCommerce specifically, the order-lookup step is the one most stores underestimate — it's the single biggest source of repetitive tickets and the [feature that pays for the tool](/blog/reduce-support-tickets-with-ai). Get it [capturing leads](/blog/capture-leads-with-conversational-chat) when it can't fully answer, and you've turned a cost centre into a channel.

## Frequently asked questions

### Does an AI chatbot work with WooCommerce products and orders?

Yes. Tools like Loqara connect through the WooCommerce REST API to search your live catalog and look up a customer's order status after a quick identity check. Native WordPress plugins read the same data directly inside WordPress. Either way, the agent answers questions around your real store data rather than guessing.

### Will the chatbot make up answers about my products or policies?

A grounded chatbot won't. Loqara only answers from the content you give it and cites its sources; when it isn't sure, it captures the lead or hands off to a human instead of guessing. Ungrounded, model-only bots are the ones that invent shipping windows and return policies — exactly what you want to avoid on a store.

### How much does an AI chatbot for WooCommerce cost?

It depends on the pricing unit. Loqara is priced per conversation with a free tier of 100 conversations a month; helpdesk suites like Gorgias and Zendesk bill per AI "resolution" on top of seat fees; native plugins are cheap upfront but run on your own hosting. Model your real monthly volume against each unit before committing, because the same traffic can cost very differently.

### Should I use a WordPress plugin or a SaaS chatbot?

Use a native plugin if you want a simple, low-cost FAQ widget and don't need strong AI resolution. Use a SaaS tool when you want grounded answers, live product and order actions, and a proper human handoff — and don't mind a monthly subscription. Because WooCommerce is open, you can start with one and switch later without re-platforming.

### Can customers talk to a WooCommerce chatbot by voice?

With Loqara, yes — it includes an optional real-time [voice agent](/blog/voice-ai-for-ecommerce-support) in English and Lithuanian, so customers can speak their question and hear answers. Most other tools on this list are text-only today, so voice is a genuine differentiator if your audience prefers talking.

### How long does it take to set up an AI chatbot on WooCommerce?

A grounded agent with a one-line embed can be live the same day: add your content, connect WooCommerce, customise the widget, and paste one snippet into your theme. Heavier helpdesk suites take longer because there's more machinery to configure, and native plugins are quick to install but may need tuning to answer well.
