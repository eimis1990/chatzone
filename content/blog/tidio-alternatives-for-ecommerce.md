Tidio is one of the most popular live-chat tools for small businesses, and its visual flow builder is genuinely nice to use. But once you actually want **AI** answering customers, the economics change — and that's usually when stores start looking around. Here's an honest take on the alternatives.

## Why stores outgrow Tidio

Two reasons come up again and again:

1. **AI is a separate add-on.** Tidio's AI agent (Lyro) isn't really part of the base plans. The free tier includes a small, *one-time* batch of AI conversations (around 50 total, not per month), and after that the Lyro add-on starts around $39/mo for 50 conversations at the time of writing. So the headline "free" plan isn't an AI plan.
2. **The pricing cliff.** Tidio's tiers jump steeply — from a ~$59/mo plan to a ~$749/mo plan with little in between. That's a tough gap for a growing store to bridge.

None of this makes Tidio bad — it's a solid live-chat product. It just means if your goal is *AI that handles real volume*, the all-in cost can climb faster than expected.

## What to look for instead

- **AI in the core, not bolted on** — you shouldn't pay twice to get the main feature.
- **An ongoing free tier** — a monthly allowance you can actually rely on, not a one-time trial bucket.
- **Predictable steps up** — plans that grow with you, without a 10× cliff.
- **Grounded answers + real store actions** — [order lookup and catalog search](/blog/ai-chatbot-for-online-store), not just scripted flows.
