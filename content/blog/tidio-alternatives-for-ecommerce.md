Tidio is one of the most popular live-chat tools for small businesses, and for good reason: its visual flow builder is genuinely pleasant to use, the widget looks clean, and you can be answering customers within an hour. The friction shows up later. So stores outgrow Tidio not because it's bad, but because they came for chat and now need *grounded AI included*, not bolted on. Here's an honest look at the alternatives — including where staying on Tidio is the right call.

## When Tidio is enough — and when to switch

Tidio is a legitimately good product for a specific stage. Be honest about where you are before switching anything.

**Tidio is enough when** you mostly want live chat with a human behind it, you enjoy mapping conversations in a visual builder, your automation needs are light (greetings, lead capture, routing), and your volume is low enough that a one-time batch of AI replies covers the occasional natural-language question.

**You've outgrown Tidio when** any of these start to bite:

- You want AI handling *real* volume, and the Lyro add-on cost climbs as traffic grows.
- You're maintaining flows by hand and customers keep asking things your flows don't cover.
- You need the bot to **look up an order** or **search the live catalog**, not just match keywords.
- The tiers feel like a cliff — a comfortable plan, then a sudden 10x jump with little in between.

If two or more ring true, you're shopping for an AI-first tool. The honest framing: with Tidio you can end up [paying twice](/blog/how-to-choose-ai-support-agent) — once for the chat platform, again for the AI add-on — to get the feature you came for.

## How to choose your Tidio alternative

Match the tool to your stage, not the longest feature list:

- **You want grounded AI (and maybe voice) included, with predictable cost** → Loqara.
- **You're a busy multichannel team that lives in an inbox** → Gorgias.
- **You want a classic shared inbox with native order actions and lighter AI** → Re:amaze.
- **You're a larger org happy in a premium suite** → Intercom or Zendesk.
- **You mostly want a cheap, simple inbox** → Crisp.
- **You love hand-built flows and don't need much AI yet** → stay on Tidio.

Two practical checks before you switch. First, run the numbers on your *actual* monthly volume against each pricing unit — the same traffic can cost very differently under "per conversation" versus "per resolution." Second, confirm the tool can natively [look up an order](/blog/reduce-support-tickets-with-ai) and search your live catalog — the two things that quietly cause most tickets. If it can't, you'll be back to maintaining flows by hand. ## Frequently asked questions

### Is Tidio's Lyro AI included for free?

Not in the way most people expect. Tidio's free plan includes only a small, one-time batch of AI conversations rather than a recurring monthly allowance, and after that Lyro is a separate paid add-on layered on top of your plan. If your goal is AI that handles ongoing volume, budget for the add-on or pick a tool where AI is included in the base plan.

### What is the best Tidio alternative for AI support?

For most e-commerce stores, Loqara is the closest match because AI is included rather than sold as an add-on. It gives grounded answers with citations, searches your catalog, looks up orders, and adds an optional voice agent — with a real free tier of 100 conversations a month. Larger support teams may prefer Gorgias or Intercom, which are heavier but built for high-volume multichannel ticketing.

### Is there a Tidio alternative with a real free tier?

Yes. Loqara's free plan includes 100 conversations every month — a recurring allowance, not a one-time trial bucket — so you can run real traffic through it before paying. That differs from free plans that hand you a fixed lifetime number of AI replies and then stop, which is what catches stores off guard.

### Do these chatbots connect to WooCommerce, Shopify, and Magento?

It varies by tool, so check each one's app listing. Loqara connects to WooCommerce, Shopify, and Magento, plus a product feed for other platforms, so the agent can search live products and look up orders inside the chat. Helpdesk suites like Gorgias and Zendesk integrate with the major platforms too, though the depth of native store actions differs.

### Can a Tidio alternative answer by voice?

With Loqara, yes — it includes an optional real-time voice agent so customers can speak their question and hear an answer, in English or Lithuanian. Tidio and most other tools on this list are text-only today, so if spoken support matters to your store, it's a genuine point of difference.

### Should I just stay on Tidio?

If you mostly want live chat with hand-built flows and light automation, and you love the drag-and-drop builder, staying on Tidio is a perfectly reasonable choice. The only question worth answering honestly is whether you're paying for AI you're not fully getting — if AI add-on fees and pricing cliffs keep biting, a tool with AI included will usually be cheaper and simpler.
