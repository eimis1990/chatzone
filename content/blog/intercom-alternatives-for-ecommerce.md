## When Intercom and Fin are worth it — and when they're overkill

Intercom earns its price in a specific environment. If you have a real support team, a complex product that needs onboarding and proactive messaging, and enough conversation volume that an outcome-based AI fee is easy to forecast, Fin is hard to beat. Its [clean handoff to a human](/blog/ai-chatbot-human-handoff), reporting depth, and workflow builder are mature in a way newer tools aren't.

The trouble is the pricing *model*, not the sticker price. Intercom bills per seat for the platform and Fin bills per resolution — roughly a dollar per resolved conversation at the time of writing, with a reported resolution rate in the mid-70s percent. That's a fair, outcome-based deal that's genuinely predictable once you're at scale. But for a lean store it's two meters running at once: a platform cost *and* a usage cost you have to forecast on traffic you can't fully predict.

Intercom isn't "too expensive" in a vacuum — it's priced for a different buyer. The honest question is whether you're that buyer *yet*.

## What should I look for in a store-shaped alternative?

If you're moving away from Intercom because it's SaaS-shaped, the replacement should be store-shaped. Four things matter more than feature count:

- **Built around catalog and orders**, not product tours and seat-based workflows. ([What a good store agent actually does.](/blog/ai-chatbot-for-online-store))
- **Predictable pricing** — a flat plan with a clear allowance beats stacking a platform fee and a per-resolution meter you have to model every month.
- **Grounded answers plus real actions** — live order lookup and product search, not just FAQ keyword matching that invents a returns window when it's unsure.
- **Handoff on every plan** — a clean path to a human that isn't gated behind the top tier.

## How to choose your Intercom alternative

Match the tool to your stage, not the longest feature list:

- **Small or growing store, want AI plus maybe voice, one predictable number** → Loqara.
- **You enjoy building flows by hand and will add AI later** → Tidio.
- **Busy multichannel team that lives in an inbox and has the volume for per-resolution AI** → Gorgias.
- **Mid-size, want a classic inbox with native order actions** → Re:amaze.
- **Already standardised on a big suite company-wide** → Zendesk (or stay on Intercom).
- **Tiny team, tightest budget, just need a shared inbox** → Crisp.

The deciding factor is almost always the pricing *model*, not the headline number — a per-resolution fee behaves very differently from a flat plan at your volume. ## Frequently asked questions

### What is the best Intercom alternative for a small e-commerce store?
For a lean store, a store-shaped agent like Loqara is usually the better fit because it's built around your catalog and orders rather than SaaS product tours, installs with one line, and is priced per conversation with a free tier of 100 a month. Intercom and Fin remain excellent, but they're designed and priced for larger support organisations. Match the tool to your stage, not the longest feature list.

### Why is Intercom so expensive for online stores?
Intercom charges per seat for the platform and Fin charges per resolution on top, so you're running two cost meters at once. That's a fair, outcome-based model once you have the volume and a dedicated team to justify it, but for a small store it means paying for a SaaS feature surface — product tours, complex workflows, big seat counts — you may never use.

### Is Fin AI better than other AI chat agents?
Fin is genuinely one of the strongest AI agents available, with a high reported resolution rate and excellent tooling, so on raw capability it's hard to fault. The trade-off is that it's premium and per-resolution. Lighter grounded agents built for commerce can match it for store-specific jobs — order lookup, product search, policy answers — at a far more predictable price.

### What is the difference between per-resolution and per-conversation pricing?
A resolution is a vendor-defined outcome that can be counted more than once per customer, while a conversation is a single customer thread regardless of how it ends. On the same traffic, per-resolution billing can cost three to four times more than per-conversation billing. Always model your real monthly volume against each unit before committing.

### Can an Intercom alternative look up orders and search my catalog?
Yes — that's exactly what a store-shaped agent should do. Loqara connects to WooCommerce, Shopify, and Magento (plus a product feed) to search your live catalog and look up a customer's order after a quick identity check. That order-lookup ability is what actually [deflects the repetitive tickets](/blog/reduce-support-tickets-with-ai), which FAQ-only bots can't touch.

### Do cheaper Intercom alternatives still hand off to a human?
The good ones do, on every plan. Loqara includes a shared-inbox live handoff so a person can step in without the customer repeating themselves, and tools like Gorgias and Re:amaze are built around a human inbox by design. Be wary of any tool that gates handoff behind its top tier.
