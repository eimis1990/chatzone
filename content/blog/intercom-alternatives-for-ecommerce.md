Intercom is one of the most polished support platforms around, and its Fin AI agent is genuinely strong — it resolves a high share of conversations end to end. But Intercom is built for large SaaS and support organisations, and the price reflects that. If you're running an online store, here's an honest look at the lighter alternatives.

## Why stores look past Intercom

It usually comes down to two things:

1. **The price point.** Intercom is a premium suite. For a lean store, you're often paying for a feature surface (product tours, complex workflows, large seat counts) you won't use.
2. **Per-resolution AI billing.** Fin charges per resolution — around $0.99 each at the time of writing, with a reported ~76% resolution rate. That's a fair, outcome-based model and predictable at scale, but for a small store it's another usage meter to forecast on top of the platform cost.

Intercom isn't "too expensive" in a vacuum — it's priced for a different buyer. The question is whether you're that buyer yet.

## What to look for instead

- **Store-shaped, not SaaS-shaped** — built around catalog and orders, not product tours and seat-based workflows. ([What a good store agent does.](/blog/ai-chatbot-for-online-store))
- **Predictable pricing** — a flat plan with a clear allowance beats stacking a platform fee and a per-resolution meter.
- **Grounded answers + real actions** — order lookup and product search, not just FAQ matching.
- **Handoff on every plan** — [a clean path to a human](/blog/ai-chatbot-human-handoff), not gated behind the top tier.
